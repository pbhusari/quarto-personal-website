Street Tree Monitoring Network (STEM)

May 2021 – Summer 2024 · DevPost

🏆 1st Place — Microsoft Azure IoT Hack for Sustainability (Global)

Built a deployment-ready demo in 48 hours. Endorsed by San José city arborists and backed by peer-reviewed urban forestry research.

Urban forests are critical infrastructure — but newly-planted street trees have up to 30% annual mortality, largely from inadequate watering during the first few years of establishment. STEM is a city-scale IoT sensor network that gives urban foresters real-time soil moisture visibility across every monitored tree, turning reactive tree care into data-driven stewardship.

What started as a hackathon project became a real deployment. After winning globally, I took STEM from prototype to a working local network — securing Microsoft for Startups backing and $10,000 from the City of Boulder, Colorado to fund the first sensor rollout. I continued developing the system as a Purdue directed project, spending the summer of 2024 on the hardware side: designing and building out a LoRaWAN gateway and conducting a rigorous embedded system power study to validate the 5-year battery life target for field-deployed sensor nodes.

Each node is a capacitive moisture sensor paired with an ESP32 microcontroller, potted in vandalism-resistant PVC housing for underground installation. Readings stream over MQTT → LoRaWAN gateway → Azure IoT Hub → Stream Analytics → SQL Server, surfaced through an ASP.NET Core dashboard with Azure Maps and Charts.js for geospatial and historical views.
